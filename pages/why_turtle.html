Why TurtleCoin?


Turtlecoin:

Launched on December 9, 2017 as an open source, community-driven coin. The community is open and accessible, and it's a fun coin to mine. Rather than Bitcoin (cappped at 21 Million) or Ethereum (uncapped) Turtle decided to cap at 1 TRILLION coins.

This pool is owned by @papacabeza and managed by @thedepressor. Our collaborationn began on the Turtlecoin Discord channel and our collaboration agreement was paid for in Turtle.


Why are we building a Turtlecoin pool?

  1. Turtlecoin is truly community driven. Turtlecoin isn't a security, and is demonstrating that it has utility. The "TurtleCoin" is merely a program running on the TRTL Network. TRTL propels things like smart contracts on the TRTLE sidechain, similiar to ETH. Turtle's community-driven mission will reduce that risk. [See the Medium installments and Discord channel]
  2. Strong lead developer. The success of some of the most important open-source-like-projects depends on a strong community and an even stronger, charismatic (sometimes maniacal) vision-driven lead developer. Examples: Linus Torvalds + Linux; or Jimmy Wales + Wikipedia; or Craig Newmark + Craigslist. The TurtleCoin lead, @RockSteady has that character. [See Cathedral and the Bazaar, the basis for this criterion].
  3. ASIC resistance. Turtle is commited to mining decentralization and has already forked it’s code in order to avoid ASIC takeover of the algo. [See this Medium article explaining the philosophy].
  4. Follow the leaders. Turtle has strong early support from some key figures in crypto, and they’ve endorsed it with no quid pro quo, no bounty. [See Kevin Rose’s podcast]


Why join our pool?
